about

I'm a second year PhD student in computer science at Harvard in the Statistical Reinforcement Learning Lab. I'm interested in understanding and addressing the challenges people face when applying reinforcement learning algorithms to real world problems. I am currently working on statistical inference methods for adaptively collected data, e.g. data collected using a bandit. I am advised by Susan Murphy and Lucas Janson. I am fortunate to have received a NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.

I previously worked on natural language processing problems with Sasha Rush and was a member of the Natural Language Processing group. During my undergrad at NYU, I was a member of the Machine Learning for Language Lab (ML²) at CILVR, and was advised by Sam Bowman and Yann LeCun.
